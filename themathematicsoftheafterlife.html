A The Mathematics of the Afterlife

Tweet


As far as i'm aware, life after death is only a concept that has received extensive attention from religion. Setting aside imortalisation in the annals of human history, science has failed to yield any evidence, be it direct or circumstantial to even hint at the possibility of life after death.

If I were to tackle this problem (assuming the premise to be true) i'd start by building a case at the lowest levels of human disciplines - Mathematics and philosophy. If a successful, stable framework could be built, tested and remains internally consistent on this level, then a promotion to the realms of physics would be the next step.

Note, this is precisely where string Theory is right now: mathematically it yields simplicity, beauty and a hypothetical mechanism to unify classical and quantum physics in a paradigm that would tie a bow on the theory of Everything. From a physics standpoint it's the holy grail, from a philosophical standpoint, it conforms tightly with the 'physics intutions' claimed by experts and non-experts since the 1700s - it feels pretty and simple, so it must be right...right?

The jury is still out on this, though skepticism is rising, with many lamenting the decades lost in the pursuit of something so hard to validate. It would require a particle accelerator the size of jupiter to drill down and observe some of the phenomena predicted.

From this vantage point, it's hard not to be a bit perplexed, we spent billions on the LHC and machines to validate string theory based on a set of refined mathematical principles - would we be so quick to do the same for mathematical principles that had the same beauty and simplicity, but predicted the existence of a soul? I doubt it.

Like in terms of value to humanity, the ability to prove we do in fact live on would be a far bigger discovery than taking the next step in high energy physics. The impact on society, religion and culture would be wide spread, possibly the biggest news ever, even more than an eventual first contact.
