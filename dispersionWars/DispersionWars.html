🇬🇧 Welcome! This is the opening chapter of Dispersion Wars, an action Sci-Fi which follows Allan Forbes: a specialist Mech Tech in UK-27 an elite team assigned to investigate one of many Alien M.O.U.N.D Structures that have been appearing all over the globe. Full summary.

Dispersion Wars

 

“The desire for safety stands against every great and noble enterprise.”

- Tacitus, 56-117 AD.

 

-1-

 

The first time I witnessed a MOUND event was during a weekday, I think it was a Wednesday, fortunately I was on vacation, so it didn’t really matter. I was drinking at a small pub in a place called Farnell; a village in North East Scotland. 

Summer was coming to an end and it was getting dark outside, I didn’t care much, beer, entertainment and accommodation was all paid for by the UNCC United Nations Central Command. The only requirement they told me was to check in, wait for the call and don’t go far – fine by me.

 

         “Allan, your turn mate” said Miguel, passing me a crooked pool queue that we were sharing. Miguel was a tech specialist assigned to my team; we made friends online earlier and decided to hang out at the same pub before being deployed. He was a bit juvenile, but then again so was I, we got on just fine.

As I was taking my shot, Miguel continued talking

“Anyways, I have been here for 5 days now, there is frig all to do in town, I am not a fan of just sitting and waiting for something that may or may not happen.”

“Yea man, but think of it this way, with everything that’s going on right now it’s quite a nice change of pace to be here; before things really kick-off” I replied as if tempting fate. 

Just like that, both our phones started ringing, we looked at each other as if we had just witnessed a ghost sink the eight-ball. 

“You fucker” said Miguel jabbing me in the shoulder with his index finger, I let out a defeated sigh and reached for my phone.

         “Hello”

         “Please confirm identity” said a disjointed female robot. 

I turned the phone to my face whilst holding my finger over the fingerprint scanner.

 

         “A.L.L.A.N F.O.R.B.E.S Identity confirmed, please hold”

 

After about 2 seconds I was connected to my commanding officer Banes, who was a 24/7 brass tacks man.

 

         “It’s happening, log in to the UN coordinator app for briefing updates and meeting details, but first I need you two to go check it out since you are the nearest”.

“Check what out?” I replied, 

 Just then we heard sounds I will never forget; a dull, heavy warbling thrum reverberated outside, similar to that of a WW2 bomber but it sounded as if it was coming from all directions. What differentiated it was the frequency, now it was all over the place speeding up and slowing down, the pitch also began to oscillate wildly…then it was gone. 

         “Ah good, I can hear it from here; the M.O.U.N.D is in Rossie Moor a patch of farmland not far from your current location. Drive on over, cordon off the area and see if you can check it out visually, but don’t go inside! You got that?” said Banes. 

         “Uhm…”, I hesitated.

         “What is it?” Banes said impatiently.

         “Well, I thought we still had days and am on my third pint, I think”

         “Fuck sake Forbes…. fine jump a taxi or pay someone to give you a lift, hell, run there if you like, it’s only two miles away. I don’t care just get there pronto”.

         “Will do sir”, he hung up before I could finish the sentence. 

         

Miguel must have got the same pep talk judging by the look on his face, we grabbed our stuff, updated the UNC app and set off. Fortunately, there was already a taxi waiting outside for us, nice touch.

 

 We turned up at Rossie Moor about ten minutes later, fortunately no one was around, it was the middle of nowhere after all. The locals from nearby towns were well aware of these events, as such curiosity had long petered out. Also, governments threatening to impose serious fines and imprisonment for ‘trespassing’ sure helped matters. 

 

 The sun was almost set, but there was still just enough light from our phones and the setting sun to walk through the fields without too much stumbling. We were loosely following the coordinates shown on the app, this MOUND was designated UK-71-U. This type of abbreviation was given to a series of structures that had appeared all over the world, in this case: it was in the UK sector, the 71st occurrence and labelled with a U for unregistered. I knew other labels existed, such as I for inactive or B for benign; I had admin privileges to make changes to the designation, I hoped that I would never need to use the designation M, which meant Malignant. 

 

Just then we saw it… and smelled it.

 

 A pungent sulphur like smell drew us to an otherwise-normal hill in the middle of nowhere, but this one had sprouted an abnormal protrusion which looked like the hill had grown a wart overnight. Given the rugged surface of these anomalies, the term wart became commonplace and frankly was preferable to ‘Massive Oblique Unidentified Non-Newtonian Deformation M.O.U.N.D’.

 

“Target located” I said to no one in particular.

“Smells like your breath” quipped Miguel, I could tell he was trying to put a brave spin on it. Think you are a brave? Remember that next time you are in a field in the pitch dark next to a giant alien tit. 

 

“Miguel, give Banes a call, I’m going to check it out” I said.

“I deserved that” replied Miguel with a grin, I returned the smile and got to work.

 

 The wart looked like a huge giant had pinched at the hill, pulling it upwards like it was a piece of soggy dough, then letting it go to form into an unequal clump. I estimated the structure to be around 50m2 and 3 meters in height. Interestingly the mound was made from the same matter as its surroundings; I knew a partially transparent patch could be seen in the daylight, this and others like it around the globe was determined to be an entrance. I took care not to fall in. 

 

After a couple of hours of fumbling we called it a night, there were 200 warts around the world being investigated; we headed back to the pub, this one could wait a bit longer. 

 

 The next day we were at the scene bright and early, there were several military tents, jeeps and even a ferry-drone that had brought the rest of our team over. Our team of five were part of a newly formed rapid response UN unit hailing from the EU sector, each unit was assigned to a separate wart structure. 

 

 After several hours of waiting and drinking coffee we were finally given clearance to enter. 

 

There was a small but noticeable resistance when moving through the partially transparent entrance, but inside was even more peculiar. It felt like we were in a cave, but the light from outside did not disperse through the whole room, instead it tapered into a small triangle on the floor as if it was being sucked into a black hole. There was however some ambient light being emitted from the large glowing symbols on the walls, not being able to see any discernible power source, I decided to check it out.  

 

Meanwhile David our team leader was working on what appeared to be a doorway directly opposite the entrance that we came through. “Fuck, it won’t budge. There is a small hole though, let’s try to poke it with something long and thin.” Said David. 

 

 “Use your dick”, said Miguel. 

 

 Laughter ensued throughout the team, I was glad they were keeping in good spirits…and that David didn’t drop Miguel outright. 

 

Ella, a young talented biochemist was pushing up against the entrance that we just came through, it had apparently sealed up yet was still letting light in. She gestured to the rest of the team.

 

“So, what do we do now? Why do you think the drones made it in and out?”.  

“No idea” I replied, “maybe it’s possible the entrance seals up after a set number of pass-throughs?”.

 

 I was assigned to the team as a mech-tech, the equivalent of a combat engineer and it was my job to assess any signs of technology, but already I was feeling out of my depth. 

 

“It could be that it’s some sort of barrier that sensed biological life and bagged us like a Venus fly trap” retorted Ella. 

“Yea I thought of that too, let’s try not to piss our pants just yet though.” 

“Any luck David?” I asked, preferring to look for solutions. 

“Kind of, it feels like I’m picking a padlock, I definitely have traction on the pin, it’s just…”

“Just what?” I asked. 

 

David went silent, removed the pick, turned around and looked at the team with a blank expression. 

 

“What the fuck David?” exclaimed Miguel noticing that something wasn’t quite right. 

“Guys! Look sharp!” he continued, signalling towards the rest of us, we instantly complied raising and pointed our guns towards David. 

 

 David was the assigned leader, military trained, even although he was only a Major in the British Army, he secured this job due to his recent experience with a similar wart structure found in Wales. David was the last to enter back then, found everyone unconscious and dragged each of them out, one at a time.

 

With a deadpan face David started for Ella, as he sprinted the rest of the team began firing, except nothing happened. 

 

“Shoot him, my fucking gun’s jammed” exclaimed Miguel. 

“Mines too, shit!” replied a panicked Ella. 

 

 Thinking fast I stuck out my foot, tripping David; with no time to recover he carried his momentum, then fell. Wham! his face hit the ground hard, jaw first as the side of his face and elbows skidded along the rugged surface making the whole team wince in unison. 

 

I quickly followed up, putting my boot on David’s back and snapping my gun in anticipation of resistance, but there was none. Just like that David stopped moving. 

 

“What the fuck, is he conscious?” I exclaimed.

Miguel began inching slowly towards David 

“David, what’s going on? Charlotte, check him out.”

 

Charlotte, the competent all-rounder in the team was a small build, black combat medic and was one of the first members assigned to the team. This was in response to the Wales incident that mandated all teams to have medical support. 

 

“His pupils are dilated and sluggish, he looks out of it” 

“David, can you hear me? Are you alright?” she asked. 

David raised his head to look at Charlotte as if awaking from a hangover.

         “Charlotte? What happened?”

         “It’s alright, can you remember anything? You went full fuck nugget on us for a moment there” 

         “I…I remember messing about with the door, that’s about it. I must have blacked out”.

Charlotte gestured for me to let David get up, I did so with hesitation. 

Realizing I was next in command I began issuing orders like there was no tomorrow, 

“Charlotte, fix him up, also let’s report this back to central, sorry David I’m going to have to bind your hands for a bit to reduce the unknown variables, hope you understand mate”. 

         “Yea…ok”

         “Cheers, oh and good job not bitching about your injuries” I said giving Miguel a wink.

         “Get fucked” Miguel quipped. That at least put a smile on David’s face. 

 

I began getting down to business, working on the portable equipment we brought in with us. All teams investigating warts were supplied with ‘science crates’, essentially a metal box of tricks including oscilloscopes, multi-meters, comms equipment and more. The only problem was that half the gear was faulty or dead altogether. 

 

         “Guys I don’t think our video feeds are getting through, I tried contacting base but got frig all. I’ve been thinking, this probably isn’t a trap, but it does suggest the creators are likely to be hostile”.

         “How so?” asked David, who was by now sitting on the side of the box licking his wounds. 

         “Well, I’m guessing the doorway that messed with you has some kind of friend or foe recognition, that’s probably what made you go shitballs” I explained.

         “That’s conjecture mate”

         “Yea but think about it, we are likely to be in something similar to an airlock. The outer door can afford to swing open now and again, but the inner door has to be strictly locked down”

         “Actually, that makes sense, but the outer door we came through seems to be opaque now” said Charlotte. 

         “That’s where the hostile bit comes in, someone clearly wants to come through to our side but prevent us from going the other way. Maybe he or she has an evil corporate key card or something”.

 

Trying to hide his concern, David shuffled towards me, this time giving me his full attention. 

 

         “Hmm, ok, that definitely makes sense, but it doesn’t necessarily mean they are hostile. It’s probably just an advanced airlock, to ensure it’s not accidentally opened by flying debris or something”.

         “True, but that’s why you would put a failsafe on the door – to fuck up any biological entity trying to come back through that isn’t on their team” I said with a grimace on my face, half realising the gravity of what I just said. 

 

          “Fuuuuck, sounds like it is a trap then?” Charlotte exclaimed, nodding towards Ella in recognition, given she originally called it. 

 

         “Uh, yea, yea I guess so” I replied. 

 

David made a spinning motion with his index finger, “Right, I’ve heard enough, we’re scrubbing this, let’s get out ASAP we need to report this. Ella, I need you to…what the…weren’t you listening? get back from there! You saw what happened to me!”. 

 

 At this point Ella was right next to the doorway as though she was struggling to peer through a fogged-up window trying to make out what was on the other side. Ella’s expression suddenly changed, her eyes widened as she slowly stepped back 

 

“Guys, I think someone’s coming”.

 

 Just then a silhouette formed on the doorway and it wasn’t shaped like a human, for one thing, I was counting four arms or appendages. I could hear a faint humming noise and I was pretty sure I could feel static in the air, just then the weirdest thing happened. The door didn’t open, rather a tiny black circle appeared on the surface, it expanded until the recess was the size of the doorway, it seemed to then fall back on itself as if eating out a chunk from the wall. 

 

 In the black void stood a figure what could best be described as a humanoid body with a head resembling that of an alligator, except the skin on the face was a metallic silver. It was holding a metal rod of sorts and appeared to have lots of items or equipment attached to its clothing I couldn’t tell if it was tech, ceremonial attire or armour, I was dreading it was the latter. 

 

 My mum used to say that most people who look violent, usually are. It took us all of 10 seconds to test this hypothesis, after shouting and signalling for it to drop the rod, it looked at us and began talking. Bizarrely what we assumed to be its mouth didn’t move. At first, we heard random gibberish, then we could make out a single word being verbalised, slowly and laboured as if speaking for the first time.

 

“S   C    U    M”

 

 “I don’t think that’s alien speak for hello” I said aloud. Just then out of nowhere I felt a violent force pulling me downward, being a 6ft guy I can hold my own, in this case however, I crumpled to the ground like Jenga. 

 

 My face was smushed tight to the ground, it took tremendous effort just to raise my head, but I managed it; looking around I could see the rest of the team didn’t fare any better. We were all stuck to the ground, as if gravity had just increased 5 fold, looking at croc man or whatever the hell he was, I could see he was fidgeting with the rod device, I imagined he was typing in a new recipe of pain for us.
 

Angry and desperate I spoke without thinking, (a daily occurrence for me), “fucking Pussy” I jibbed. 

The alien turned its attention to me, 

“Interesting, you talk about mating with felines in your last moments. Primates are so perplexing”. 

 

Being completely oblivious to the psychology of croc men, I decided anger might be a universal trait, and that it was a theory worth testing. 

 

         “No, I mean you are a coward, a big guy like you has to force us to the ground, you must be really scared of us.”

         “What are you babbling about human? I’m not here to fight you. That’s beneath me, we have slaves for that. I am an emissary assigned to deliver the terms of your planet’s capitulation, to your leaders in person”. 

         “Ok, ok I understand, you’re chicken, don’t worry we won’t tell anyone. Anyway, I’m sure the guys watching this back at central won’t think you are running away. You are after all a grand emissary”, when I bullshit, I always like to go extra-large with a side serving of fuck you. 

 

         “Insolence, fine I will grant you your wish, I will carry your entrails with me as a trinket for your leaders, anyway, it should serve to better clarify our intentions”. 

 

         Our Croc-man friend pointed his wand of misery in my direction, and I instantly felt the pressure alleviate, I got up, noticing the rest of my team were still pinned to the floor. I was fired up and for a moment I really considered going for it, but a quick re-evaluation of the aliens size drove some stark realities home. Even although I was 6ft he still towered over me by at least another 3 feet. I decided to stall for time.

         “What’s your name? Why come out all this way, we must be pretty valuable to you” 

         “Scum, you know nothing, your world is just a single item on a long list.”

         “Hah, I get it, you are just a servant, maybe at your level, name and rank are so meaningless it may as well be omitted”. 

 

 It was my experience, that the best way to piss off arrogant people with power, was to question the basis of their power. Today I was in luck, well sort off; croc-man pivoted sideways and lunged for me with its two right arms, instantly feeling the unfairness of nature, I darted backwards dodging most of the attack. One of its claws caught a buckle on the side of my uniform ripping it out and spinning me around, I fell the ground but quickly righted myself in preparation for its next play. 

 

         “I’ve changed my mind, I’m going to keep you as my pet, I will enjoy torturing every last increment of your flesh” 

 

         Running out of ideas, my eyes darted around the room frantically looking for something I could use, just then I noticed David pinned down next to my feet, I don’t normally carry a combat knife, but David sure does. I urgently groped my very confused senior officer and found what I was looking for. Not liking my odds in hand to claw combat, I decided to go for gold, I twirled the knife in my fingers and threw it with as much force as I could muster at the startled being. 

 

The alien let out a deafening whale, even though it was exactly what I was hoping for, the deep warbling growl was pretty disconcerting. Refocusing my vision, I could see I had nailed Mr croc right in the eyeball, unlike crocodiles on earth, its eyes were more central and bulbous. I couldn’t believe my luck; I was aiming for anywhere on the face and didn’t expect to hit the bullseye so to speak. 

 

 Now however wasn’t time to celebrate, I needed to capitalise on my luck, I dove in close and grabbed the metallic wand-like object. Retreating back to the other corner of the room, I was on borrowed time and was about to bash buttons when I noticed there were six circles, four of which were inside red boxes. 

 

I quickly figured out what the red boxes meant, now I just needed to guess which of the two circles was me and which was our croc friend.  Assuming naturally the nearest one was myself; I tapped the other circle and boy was it a sight to see. The alien was instantly and violently pulled to the ground, for an added bonus, it fell face first; this had the unintended effect of driving the knife further into its skull...if it even had one. It made a subdued gargling noise as fluids gushed out its face, the sight was not pretty. 

 

Now came the hard part, looking at the device, I didn’t know if tapping the red squares would remove the gravity field or up the Auntie and turn my team into pancakes. Well aware of their agony I had to act, I tapped the next square closest to me and David slumped out on his back his body sprawled over the ground. At first, I thought I had killed him, but I was soon relieved when I heard him groan and curse. I repeated the process for everyone else, and soon we were back on our feet again, except for David, he seemed to be taking it much worse than the rest of us.   

 Whilst we gathered ourselves Ella noticed the barrier was no longer in effect, we quickly exited the mound deciding it was best to report back to central.

 

Once I was able, I called to Banes:

 “Colonel, we are back from wart UK-21 and have some urgent intel to update you on” I said. 

“What? Why the hell are you back so soon? Report” Banes replied in an irritable manner. 

“Uh well we kind of made first contact sir”

“What do you mean kind of? You either did or you didn’t”

“We did, but the enemy was hostile and alien”

“…”

“Sir are you there”

“Yes, so what happened? How many died?” asked Banes seeming distracted. 

“None sir, it used some very advanced technology, but we managed to get the upper hand.”

“Hmm, impressive; look we need you to go back in there, we don’t have time for mission planning or briefings. I need you to explore the tunnels, find anything of strategic value and cause some damage. Better yet, find someone of importance, and bring them back here by force.”

“Tunnels? You mean the doorway?” 

“Yes, we haven’t been able to get through, in fact much has happened since you were gone; we have begun encountering hostile resistance at a number of warts. We need to find a way to gain back some initiative”. 

“Uh ok sir, can we get a couple of hours to gear up and eat?”
 “You’ve got 30 minutes, by the way did your friend say anything before you offed him?”

“Only that we were scum and he was an emissary to our leaders to demand our surrender”

“hah, ok, try to catch them alive next time. Oh yea, when you go back in toss its body out to us and anything else you found of value”. 

I was going to reply but he had already hung up. 

         Looking at the team, I broke the news to them “looks like we are going back in” I said, they looked like I had just flushed their goldfish.

Loch Ness 

 

 

“Why are there so many warts here compared with the rest of the world?”

 

“We planned to subdue your planets most powerful leaders; this sets an example to the rest of the world. We find this eases the process of enslavement as it reduces the probability of coordinated planetary resistance. We thought this would have been obvious, don’t you do the same?” 

 

“If anything, you have united the worlds superpowers and given them more time to prepare”.

 

“Superpowers? Prepare? Aren’t you a single government planet? How can your empire splinter so quickly? Even our shortest Detronian empire lasted hundreds of planetary orbits”

 

“Are you talking about the British empire? We aren’t even in the top three global military powers now.”

 

 Satish pulled the chain softly but for Stephen this was the equivalent of a violent yanking on his metal collar, about breaking both his back and neck in the process. Just then a map of the earth was projected onto the dank grey breeze block wall. The map, if you could call it that looked like a satellite image rolled flat, with no boundaries or markings. Interestingly, Stephen noticed the map was a Mercator projection map; having a large bias to Northern the Hemisphere showing the UK, Europe, Alaska and Greenland to be much bigger than they actually are.  Satish gestured his claw to the picture.

 

         “Which parts of the map host these superpowers” 

         “Fuck you” whimpered Stephen, on all fours barely capable of breathing.

 

 Satish reached out one of his mammoth arms, effortlessly scooping up Stephen by the waist. As he brought Stephen closer to the picture, Stephen felt his ribs cracking with violent agony, he squirmed unable to breath or scream there was no respite, he prayed for it to end soon. 

Unexpectedly Stephen felt a sharp throbbing pain in his right foot, shooting up his body Stephen looked down in horror to see that his foot has been crushed to a pulp in Satish’s fingers, it was as if Sateesh had just popped a piece of bubble wrap. 

 

 “OK, I admit I got a bit carried away; I just really had hoped this campaign would have gone smoothly. Let’s do an information exchange; I will give you some background into our campaign and you give me the latest sitrep on your planet. I will even let you go, so we both leave with something of value”.

 

 Lowering him down, Stephen flopped to the ground sprawling in pain as he could feel the life slipping away from him. Sateesh then removed his left hand from his behind him revealing a small hollow tube, he slowly moved the tube over Stephens body barely touching the man. Stephen gave out a giant gasp of extasy as all the pain was instantly gone, looking at Sateesh with a newfound expression of love, it was obvious he had just been drugged, and they were working. 

 

 Sateesh began talking in a more patient and reserved tone, as if explaining a fact of nature to a child for the first time.

We soon noticed that your little island was applying the largest global influence, your ambitions, expansion and iron rule reminded us of ourselves, you should be flattered really. 

Our experts estimated it would be only 50 orbits until your planet achieved the pinnacle factor; a state of authoritarian fascist global rule and centralised dictatorship. 

 

Anyway, this prompted us to move your planet up the list for enslavement especially given your empires growing slave trade. In our experience we find that species exhibiting the pinnacle factor accepts our global subjugation much more readily since for most of the slaves, it’s mostly just a case of swapping masters. We are very considerate like that. Another reason is planets undergoing empire rule generally respond to conflict in a noble head on manner. This is good for us, as its quite expensive dealing with multiple decentralised powers or worse planets with a proclivity towards dishonourable guerrilla warfare tactics. 

 

As usual we put plans into action, we then began seeding the networks of warts as you call them in preparation for today” explained Sateesh.

 

         “Even submerged, we humans still had lots of ships back then, surely we must have spotted you at some point?” asked Stephen.

         “We don’t normally do personal visits, we sent slaves species to earth hauling sensory equipment, these ones in question are quite docile long necked animals, actually the link is telling me that our first scouting seed node isn’t too far from this one”. 

         “Hahaha, sounds like Nessy was one of your spies!”

         “Sounds like you are better calling off the invasion, we have three superpowers” Stephen pointed out USA, China and Russia on the map in that order.” Stephen said in a weak tone of confidence.

He continued, “each one of these countries are nuclear capable, but they also deal frequently with insurgents specialized in guerrilla warfare, in fact most of us would rather become freedom fighters than become enslaved”

It has long been considered that first contact with aliens would begin with us looking upwards into the heavens, we sure as hell didn't expect them to come marching out of the hills.

In the twentieth century Earth, hundreds of unexplained artificial structures begin appearing all over the planet. Some of these structures are benign and inactive, others... not so much. Earth has the distinguished pleasure of hosting the next onslaught from the Deritonian empire, we just don't know it yet.

I Allan Forbes, a frequent drunk with a mild gambling addiction was drafted into this mess because of my extensive training and my renewed commitment to turning over a new leaf, that and my £40k debt. I was tasked to investigate the structure UK-27, little did I know this would be my biggest gamble yet.
